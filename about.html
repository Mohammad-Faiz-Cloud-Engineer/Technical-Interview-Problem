About This Project

Understanding the mission and vision behind the Technical Interview Repository

Project Overview

The Technical Interview Problem Repository represents a systematic effort to organize and maintain a comprehensive collection of algorithmic problems based on actual interview patterns observed across the technology industry. Updated as of June 2026.

This project serves software engineers at all career stages, from those preparing for their first technical interview to experienced professionals seeking positions at specific organizations. The temporal organization of problems allows for strategic preparation that balances current trends with foundational knowledge.

Mission Statement

To provide a structured, research-driven resource that enables software engineers to prepare effectively for technical interviews through systematic problem categorization and temporal analysis of interview patterns.

Project Scope

The repository currently encompasses problem sets from over 400 organizations spanning multiple sectors:

  • Technology leaders and innovation companies
  • Financial technology and services organizations
  • Enterprise software providers
  • Consulting and professional services firms
  • Emerging technology companies

Maintenance and Updates

The repository undergoes regular updates to maintain accuracy and relevance. This includes:

  • Incorporation of new interview reports and patterns
  • Verification of existing problem categorizations
  • Temporal recategorization as problems age
  • Addition of new company problem sets

Legal Notice

The problem sets reflect patterns observed in publicly shared interview experiences and do not guarantee representation of current interview practices at any specific organization.
