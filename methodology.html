Research Methodology

A systematic approach to compiling and organizing technical interview problems

Data Collection Process

The problem sets are compiled through systematic analysis of multiple data sources to ensure comprehensive coverage and accuracy. Our methodology emphasizes verification and temporal relevance.

Primary Sources

  • Interview reports from online coding platforms
  • Publicly available interview experiences
  • Technical discussion forums and communities
  • Historical problem pattern analysis

Verification Standards

Each problem entry undergoes a verification process to ensure accuracy and relevance. This includes cross-referencing multiple sources and validating problem categorization.

Temporal Categorization

Problems are organized into five distinct temporal categories:

30 Days

Most recent interview patterns indicating current technical focus areas

3 Months

Quarterly trends showing sustained interview topics

6 Months

Semi-annual patterns for comprehensive preparation

Historical

Problems beyond 6 months for foundational practice

Complete Set

Aggregated collection for holistic coverage

Usage Guidelines

Strategic Preparation Approach

  1. Begin with the most recent problem set (30-day file) to understand current interview trends
  2. Progress through older problem sets to build comprehensive coverage
  3. Reference the complete problem set for holistic preparation
  4. Cross-reference multiple companies to identify common algorithmic themes

Interpretation Guidelines

Recent problems indicate current technical focus areas within organizations. Historical problems provide pattern recognition opportunities and foundational practice. The temporal distribution offers insights into evolving interview practices.

Update Cycle

The repository is maintained on a regular basis to ensure data accuracy and relevance. Updates incorporate new interview reports and verify existing problem categorizations.

Research Note

This repository represents ongoing research into technical interview patterns and algorithmic problem-solving strategies. The methodology continues to evolve based on emerging data sources and interview practice trends.
